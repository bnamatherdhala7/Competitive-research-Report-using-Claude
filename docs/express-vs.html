03

Battlecards

Exactly what to say when a prospect names a competitor — and when to qualify out.

vs. Canva

"1.6M free templates, 185M users, share a design and it becomes a Canva ad. We're the default."

Canva charges $120/year for unlimited background removal. Adobe Express does it unlimited free. Canva's AI isn't commercially safe — Firefly's is. For CC subscribers: Express is already included, zero marginal cost. For enterprise: Firefly IP indemnification means legal can approve AI-generated assets. Canva can't offer that.

Template-first users who need Canva's sheer volume. Focus on Firefly quality and safety — one IP lawsuit makes the volume argument irrelevant.

vs. Microsoft Designer

"It's already in Teams, Outlook, and PowerPoint. Why install anything?"

Designer generates images from DALL-E — no IP indemnification, no commercial use guarantee. One image used in a campaign creates legal exposure. Express uses Firefly: fully licensed, legally safe for commercial use. Also: Designer can't remove backgrounds, can't animate content, and has no brand kit. It's a prompt box, not a design tool.

Casual internal communications use. Don't fight for it — qualify out and focus on external marketing, brand, and client-facing design needs where safety matters.

vs. "Canva Habit"

"I've been using Canva for 3 years. I know where everything is."

Don't ask them to switch — ask them to compare one design. Offer: "Make the same thing in Express in 5 minutes." The quality difference from Firefly is visible immediately. Then show the price: you're paying $120/year for Canva's background removal cap. Express is $99.99/year with unlimited. The habit breaks at the moment of comparison.

Pure template-volume users with no AI or compliance needs. Let them stay and target their colleagues who do have those needs.

04

PLG Acquisition Motions

Three phases. Nine moves. Each tied to a specific competitive threat and a measurable outcome.

Now · 0–30 days

Stop losing users before they evaluate

Three immediate moves to close the awareness and onboarding gap.

1
Counters — Canva's 1.6M template volume

Add AI intent search to free tier templates

Type "Q2 product launch LinkedIn banner" → get 5 ranked Express templates. Closes the perceived template gap without building 1.4M new templates. The search layer turns 350K quality templates into an effectively infinite catalog for any use case.

Free tier template-to-first-export rate
2
Counters — Microsoft Designer's zero-friction discovery

Ship "Publish to Adobe Express" button in Photoshop export menu

Fires in-product at the exact moment a CC subscriber would otherwise open Canva. Target: 30M+ CC All Apps users who have never opened Express. The discovery moment is Photoshop's export flow — not a marketing email.

New Express activations attributed to Photoshop/Illustrator entry points
3
Counters — Canva's 3-minute onboarding benchmark

Fix the first session

Map time-to-first-export, identify the single biggest drop-off, fix only that one step this sprint. Canva benchmark: 3 minutes. Express current: 8–12 min. Every minute removed increases free-to-paid conversion. Don't redesign — diagnose and fix one step.

Time to first export (new users)
3 Months

Build the loops that bring users

Four self-sustaining acquisition mechanics — each loop makes the next user cheaper than the last.

1
Counters — Canva's sharing virality

"Made with Adobe Express" share page → "Create yours free" CTA

Every public Express link gets a viewer page with one-click "start from this template" flow. No signup until export. Canva estimates 30%+ of signups from shared designs — Express currently captures 0%. This is the highest-leverage unbuilt growth loop in the product.

Share-link viewer → Express signup rate (target: 5%+)
2
Counters — Canva Teams invite loop

Collaboration invite as team expansion loop

After sharing a project: "Your reviewer needs a free Adobe account." Track same-domain multi-user activations → Teams plan upgrade trigger. Canva's team plan growth is invite-driven — Express needs the same mechanic to convert individual activations into team plans.

Invite-to-activation rate (target: 30%+), same-domain → Teams upgrade
3
Counters — Canva's Q1 2026 AI brand compliance push

"Firefly Safe" badge on public share pages

Every design using only Firefly content shows a "Firefly Safe · IP Indemnified" badge on its public page. Legal teams approving marketing assets start requesting Express. One legal approval = departmental mandate. This badge turns every export into an enterprise sales motion at zero cost.

Enterprise accounts where adoption started with legal/compliance referral
4
Captures — ChatGPT's 800M users (Express is live, Dec 2025)

Optimize ChatGPT → Express conversion

Adobe Express is live inside ChatGPT. The integration must deliver a completed, shareable output in one session — not a link to open elsewhere. Measure prompt-to-completed-export. This is the largest unmonetized acquisition surface in the product's history.

ChatGPT → Express account creation rate (target: 8%+)
6 Months

Build the moats

Three compounding advantages that get harder to copy as more users join.

1
Moat — Canva's community content Canva can't match in quality

Express Template Marketplace

CC subscribers publish professional templates (optional rev-share). 1% of 30M CC subscribers = 300K professional templates/month. Non-CC users access templates but are prompted to upgrade. Compounds because Canva's community creates at volume — Express's community creates at professional quality. Quality per template is the defensible differentiator.

Templates published/month; signups attributed to template discovery
2
Moat — Acrobat's 500M users at zero CAC

Acrobat → Express in-product prompt at PDF export

"Make this look better before you send. Open in Adobe Express." One click — PDF cover opens in Express with brand colors applied. Acrobat has 500M+ users. Express riding that install base at zero CAC is the most capital-efficient growth lever in the Adobe portfolio. No competitor can replicate this cross-product surface.

Express activations attributed to Acrobat Studio prompts (target: 25%+ cross-usage)
3
Moat — Competitors using DALL-E/SD without this signal fall further behind quarterly

Firefly AI flywheel

Every design = training signal. Export without regeneration = positive signal. Regenerate = negative. Quarterly fine-tuning on Express-specific design contexts. Compounds: competitors using DALL-E/SD without this feedback loop fall further behind every quarter. This moat strengthens automatically as Express usage grows — it requires no additional investment to compound.

First-generation Firefly acceptance rate (target: 5% quarterly improvement)
