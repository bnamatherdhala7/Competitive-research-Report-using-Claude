## TL;DR for Product Strategy

> Canva has 265 million monthly users and 4.5 million templates. Adobe Express has roughly 350,000 templates and a fraction of Canva's install base. That template gap is the #1 conversion blocker — prospects open the tool, can't find what they need fast, and go back to Canva. The roadmap must attack this. Adobe's clearest strategic moat is the one competitors cannot copy: **Creative Cloud ecosystem integration + Firefly AI commercial safety + Acrobat Studio**. The highest-ROI PM move right now is activating the 30 million+ Creative Cloud All Apps subscribers who already have Adobe Express included in their plan but don't know it.

## 1. Market Overview

The online design tool market is splitting into two distinct segments. On one side: **professional/enterprise teams** who need brand governance, template locking, approval workflows, and compliance-grade AI generation. On the other: **casual/individual creators** who need fast, frictionless, template-rich tools for social media, presentations, and marketing materials — free or near-free.

Canva owns the second segment almost completely, with 265 million monthly users and a template library 12x larger than Adobe Express's. Adobe Express is increasingly positioned for the first segment — brand management at scale for enterprise marketing teams — but has not fully shed its consumer/freemium identity, leaving it poorly positioned in both markets.

**The April 2026 context**: Canva launched AI brand compliance features in Q1 2026, directly attacking Adobe Express's enterprise brand governance advantage. Microsoft Designer continues to expand with Copilot integration, making it increasingly capable for M365-heavy organizations at $0 incremental cost. Adobe's most important recent moves — the AI Assistant (October 2025), ChatGPT integration (December 2025), and Acrobat Studio launch (August 2025) — give Express genuine differentiation at the professional tier, but the template and distribution gap with Canva remains the product's most urgent strategic problem.

## 3. Competitor Deep Dives

### 3.1 Canva — Primary Competitive Threat

**Why they dominate against Adobe Express**:
- **Template volume**: 4.5 million templates vs Adobe Express's ~350,000 on paid plans. Users open a tool, search for a template, and if they can't find one in 30 seconds they leave. Canva wins this moment every time.
- **265 million monthly users**: Network effects compound — more users → more community content → more reason to stay. Adobe Express has no comparable community layer.
- **AI Brand Compliance (Q1 2026)**: Canva launched AI-powered brand compliance checking across team content — this directly attacks Adobe Express's primary enterprise selling point. Canva now warns teams when designs deviate from brand guidelines automatically.
- **Canva Enterprise at scale**: 40% of Canva's paid subscribers are now enterprise. Enterprise licenses growing faster than any other segment. Canva is no longer a freemium consumer tool — it's an enterprise platform with a freemium top of funnel.
- **30-day free trial**: vs Adobe Express's more limited trial experience.
- **Presentation dominance**: 46% market share in presentation software — Canva Presentations is now a credible PowerPoint alternative, expanding Canva's footprint deeper into the enterprise workflow.

**Pricing**:
| Plan | Cost |
|---|---|
| Free | $0 (1.6M+ templates, 5GB storage) |
| Pro | $14.99/user/month or $119.99/year |
| Business | $20/user/month |
| Enterprise | Custom (SSO, advanced brand controls, dedicated support) |
| Teams (up to 5) | $14.99/month base |

**Acquisition messaging against Adobe Express**: Canva rarely runs direct "switch from Adobe" campaigns. They don't need to — template volume and distribution do the work. Their messaging is "Design anything, publish anywhere" — product-led growth through sheer breadth.

**Adobe Express counter**: Canva has no Creative Cloud integration (no Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Lightroom round-trip). Canva's AI image generation does not carry Firefly's commercial safety guarantee. Canva's brand governance is reactive (checks after the fact) — Adobe Express's template locking is preventative (brand can't be broken in the first place). For enterprise marketing operations teams, the preventative model is stronger. Qualification question: "Does your team need guaranteed commercial safety for AI-generated content used in paid media?"

### 3.2 Microsoft Designer — The Free Structural Threat

**Why this is the most underestimated threat**:
Microsoft Designer is free for any Microsoft 365 subscriber — and there are over 400 million M365 users globally. Every enterprise that is already paying for M365 has a CFO asking the same question they asked about Acrobat: "Why are we also paying for a design tool?"

**What Microsoft Designer covers at $0 incremental**:
- AI image generation (DALL-E powered, GPT-4 integrated)
- Social media posts, flyers, invitations, presentations
- Background remover
- Text-to-design creation from prompts
- Export in HD
- PowerPoint integration (design slides directly)
- Teams and Outlook integration for email headers and collaboration assets

**What Microsoft Designer does NOT cover** (Adobe Express's moat):
- Creative Cloud round-trip workflows (Photoshop → Express → publish)
- Firefly AI commercial safety guarantee
- Advanced brand kit with template locking
- Video creation and editing
- Acrobat Studio integration (PDF + design in one workflow)
- Depth of template library for professional outputs
- Print-ready exports

**Pricing**:
| Plan | Cost |
|---|---|
| Free (with Microsoft account) | $0 |
| Copilot Pro | $20/month (more AI boosts, deeper M365 integration) |
| Microsoft 365 Business | $12.50–$22/user/month (Designer included) |

**Acquisition messaging**: Microsoft doesn't pitch Designer directly against Express. The threat is passive — Designer appears inside Teams, PowerPoint, and Outlook. Users discover it without evaluating it. By the time they might consider Adobe Express, they're already habituated to Designer.

**Adobe Express counter**: Qualification question for M365-heavy enterprise prospects: "When your marketing team needs to produce brand-compliant social content at scale — with locked templates, approval workflows, and AI content your legal team has cleared for paid media — how does that work in Designer today?" Designer cannot answer this well. Adobe Express can.

### 3.3 VistaCreate — Low-Cost Feature-Parity Risk

**Why they win against Adobe Express**:
- **Price**: $10/month (annual) vs Adobe Express's $9.99/month — effectively the same price, but VistaCreate includes more templates and assets in its free tier
- **Free tier**: 50,000+ static and animated templates, background remover, brand kit — all free. This matches Adobe Express's free tier depth at zero cost.
- **VistaPrint integration**: VistaCreate is owned by VistaPrint/Vistaprint Group. Users can design in VistaCreate and order printed materials directly — a workflow Adobe Express cannot offer natively.
- **Animation**: Strong animated template library for social media content — competitive with Express's animation capabilities.

**Pricing**:
| Plan | Cost |
|---|---|
| Starter (free) | $0 (50K+ templates, unlimited downloads, background remover, brand kit) |
| Pro | $13/month or $10/month (annual) |
| 14-day free trial | Full feature access |

**Adobe Express counter**: VistaCreate has no AI image generation (no Firefly equivalent), no Creative Cloud integration, no video editing, no Acrobat integration, and no enterprise brand governance features. For any user who needs AI content creation or integrates with Creative Cloud, Adobe Express wins. VistaCreate is the right comparison for price-sensitive SMBs doing basic social content.

**Who they take**: Solo creators, small businesses, and designers on VistaPrint's existing customer base. Not a threat for enterprise or CC-connected workflows.

### 3.4 Picsart — Mobile-First AI Creator Threat

**Why they're growing**:
- **Mobile-native**: Picsart is the only major competitor in this space that is genuinely mobile-first. For creators who work primarily on phone — social media managers, influencers, solo content creators — Picsart's UX is optimized for their actual workflow. Adobe Express's mobile app is secondary to its web experience.
- **20+ AI tools**: Text-to-image, background removal, AI Outpainting, AI avatars, style transfer, video enhancement — built into the product from the start, not added on. Pro plan includes 200 AI generations/day vs Adobe Express's 250/month on Premium.
- **150 million creator community**: Content sharing, remix culture, and community effects rival what Canva has built. Adobe Express has no comparable community layer.
- **Enterprise AI tier**: Picsart Enterprise offers API access for businesses to embed its AI capabilities into their own products — a B2B2C motion Adobe Express has not made.

**Pricing**:
| Plan | Cost |
|---|---|
| Free | $0 (basic tools, 5 AI generations/day) |
| Plus | $5/month annual ($13/month) — 50 AI gen/day |
| Pro | $15/month — 200 AI gen/day, brand kit (5 brands), 3 seats, 500GB storage |
| Enterprise | Custom — API access, white-label |

**Adobe Express counter**: Picsart has no Creative Cloud integration, no print-quality export controls, no Acrobat workflow, no template locking for brand governance. Firefly's commercial safety is unmatched. Picsart's AI content carries no commercial safety guarantee — a significant liability for enterprise marketing teams producing paid media content.

**Who they take**: Social media managers, solo content creators, and any segment where mobile workflow dominates. Not a threat for enterprise brand governance or CC-connected professionals.

### 3.5 Visme — Professional Output Threat (B2B Niche)

**Why they win in their segment**:
- **Infographics and reports**: Visme is the best-in-class tool for creating data visualizations, infographics, business reports, and interactive presentations — outputs Adobe Express handles only superficially.
- **Interactivity**: Visme presentations and documents can include interactive elements, embedded data, and analytics — features Adobe Express does not offer.
- **B2B positioning**: Visme's messaging targets marketing and communications teams producing professional business content (pitch decks, proposals, annual reports). This is adjacent to Adobe Express's enterprise positioning but serves a different output type.

**Pricing**:
| Plan | Cost |
|---|---|
| Basic (free) | $0 (limited projects) |
| Starter | $29/month |
| Pro | $59/month |
| Enterprise | Custom |

**Adobe Express counter**: Visme is expensive ($59/month) for what it offers. Adobe Express at $9.99/month with Creative Cloud integration, Firefly AI, and Acrobat Studio covers a broader workflow at a fraction of the cost. Visme lacks Creative Cloud integration and AI image generation. For teams already in the Adobe ecosystem, switching cost is zero to stay with Express.

**Who they take**: Marketing teams whose primary output is data-heavy reports and presentations — not social content or brand asset production.

### 3.6 Figma — Adjacent Professional Design Threat

**Why they matter for Adobe Express's roadmap**:
Figma is not a direct Adobe Express competitor day-to-day — but they represent where Adobe Express needs to go for professional brand teams. Figma is the industry standard for UI/UX and brand design systems. Enterprise brand teams that use Figma for brand guidelines, design tokens, and component libraries don't use Adobe Express for those workflows. Adobe Express is positioned downstream: take the brand assets from Figma (or Illustrator) and produce social/marketing content. The risk: as Figma expands into marketing content (Figma Slides, Figma Sites), it eats into Adobe Express's territory from the professional end.

**Pricing**:
| Plan | Cost |
|---|---|
| Starter | Free (3 files, limited) |
| Professional | $15/user/month (annual) |
| Organization | $55/user/month (annual — SSO, admin) |
| Enterprise | $90/user/month (annual — dedicated support) |

**Strategic implication**: Adobe Express's integration with Illustrator and Photoshop is its answer to Figma's brand design system play. The PM team should invest in tighter Illustrator → Express handoff workflows to capture brand teams that currently use Figma for brand governance and Express for execution content.

## 8. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) for Adobe Express

### Tier 1 — Highest Win Rate

**Creative Cloud All Apps subscribers (not yet using Express)**
- 30 million+ CC subscribers — Express is already in their plan
- They're using Canva for quick content because they don't know Express is free to them
- This is not an acquisition problem — it's an activation and awareness problem
- Message: "You already have it. Here's what it does."
- Action: In-product prompt in Photoshop/Illustrator: "Publish this to social in one click with Adobe Express"

**Enterprise marketing teams in regulated industries**
- Financial services, healthcare, pharma, legal — industries where AI-generated content in paid media carries legal risk
- Canva's Magic Studio and Picsart's AI carry no commercial safety guarantee
- Firefly's IP-indemnified generation is the only option that clears legal review
- Message: "The only AI design tool your legal team has already approved."

**Brand managers at mid-to-large companies**
- Teams producing high-volume social and marketing content that must stay on-brand
- Template locking + approval workflows = Express's preventative brand governance is stronger than Canva's reactive compliance checking
- Message: "Your brand can't break in Express. In Canva, it can."

### Tier 2 — Good Win Rate with Right Positioning

**In-house creative teams at agencies and studios**
- Already in Creative Cloud — Express as the "content production layer" on top of their Photoshop/Illustrator workflows
- Pain: producing 50+ variants of a design for different channels is manual and slow in PS/AI
- Express solves this in minutes; pitch the workflow, not the tool

**Marketing teams already using Acrobat**
- Acrobat Studio integration makes Express the natural next step
- PDF-to-design-to-social workflow is unique — no competitor can replicate it

### Tier 3 — Low Win Rate (Don't Chase)

- **Pure social content creators** (influencers, solo freelancers) — Canva wins on template volume and mobile, always. Don't compete here on features; compete on price awareness only.
- **Print designers** — No CMYK = disqualified. Fix the product or concede this segment.
- **M365-only organizations without Creative Cloud** — Microsoft Designer is free, embedded, and "good enough." Adobe's pitch here requires a strong ROI case for the Creative Cloud stack beyond design.

## 10. Differentiation Opportunities for PM Roadmap

1. **Template Discovery via AI Intent** — Don't try to build 4.5M templates. Build a search/recommendation layer that finds the right Express template from a natural language prompt ("Q2 social campaign for a fintech brand, warm tones, Instagram Stories"). Compress the template gap through intelligence, not volume. This is a 6-month build that could close the #1 conversion blocker.

2. **Firefly Safety as a Compliance Product** — The commercial safety guarantee is undermarketed. Reframe Firefly's IP indemnification as a **compliance feature**, not just an AI feature. Build a dedicated "AI-Safe for Paid Media" content designation in the Express UI — content generated by Firefly gets a badge that marketing/legal teams can filter for. This makes the invisible advantage visible.

3. **Creative Cloud Activation Inside PS and AI** — Add a persistent "Publish to Express" button in Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign. Target the 30M+ CC All Apps subscribers who don't know Express is already in their plan. This is the highest-ROI activation play available — zero acquisition cost, existing subscribers, immediate usage.

4. **Acrobat Studio as the Enterprise Entry Point** — Acrobat is deeply embedded in enterprise workflows. Route enterprise Express deals through Acrobat Studio bundling — "Add Express to your Acrobat contract for $X/user." This bypasses the Canva comparison entirely and leads with a workflow the enterprise already trusts.

5. **Mobile-First Feature Parity** — Picsart and Canva have superior mobile experiences. Social media managers, the highest-volume Express use case, work primarily on mobile. A dedicated investment in mobile UX (not just a responsive version of the web app) could recapture the mobile-first segment that currently defaults to Picsart or Canva's app.

## 12. PM Strategy — Recommended Actions

### Respond Now (This Sprint / Month)

1. **Audit and fix the top 5 reported reliability bugs** — Adobe community forums and Quora both surface specific recurring issues (image loading failures, UX regressions from October 2025 update). These are costing premium subscribers. Every bug that causes a loss to Canva is the highest-cost customer event in the funnel.

2. **Launch a "You already have Express" campaign inside Creative Cloud** — Identify CC All Apps subscribers who have never opened Adobe Express. In-product notification in Photoshop: "Turn this into a social post in one click." No new acquisition spend required — this is pure activation.

3. **Build a "Firefly-Safe for Paid Media" content badge** — Make the commercial safety guarantee visible in the UI. Legal and brand teams at enterprise accounts need to see it, not just read about it in a sales deck. This closes deals where Canva is losing to AI content liability concerns.

### Roadmap (Next Quarter)

4. **AI-powered template recommendation from intent** — Natural language template search ("summer campaign, fashion brand, TikTok format") that surfaces Express templates by workflow intent, not keyword match. This is the highest-leverage response to the template gap without building 4M more templates.

5. **Dedicated mobile app investment** — Commission a mobile-first UX project separate from the web product. Social media managers are the highest-volume use case and they work on phone. This segment is currently defaulting to Canva and Picsart's mobile apps.

6. **Acrobat Studio bundling pitch for enterprise** — Work with the Acrobat sales team to include Express in all Acrobat Enterprise renewals. Enterprise customers already trust the Acrobat brand. Express is a natural upsell that sidesteps the Canva comparison entirely.

### Strategic Bets (6 months+)

7. **Brand governance as a compliance platform** — Build toward a "Brand Compliance Center" in Adobe Express Enterprise: template locking + AI generation safety + approval workflows + audit trail for brand decisions. Position Express not as a design tool but as a brand governance platform. This is the market position Canva cannot occupy — their product-led growth model doesn't support the compliance-grade audit trail an enterprise brand manager needs.

8. **Community layer for templates and remixes** — Canva's template volume moat is built on community contributions. Adobe has 30M+ CC subscribers who could contribute Express templates. An open template marketplace — where Express users publish and remix — is a 2-year build that creates a compounding moat Canva cannot easily replicate because it would require opening their platform to CC ecosystem assets.

## 13. PLG Acquisition Strategy — Against Competitors

> Adobe Express's biggest PLG deficit is simple: Canva's product is the funnel. Every Canva design shared publicly is an ad for Canva. Every team member invited to collaborate is a new Canva user. Every template discovered on Google brings someone into Canva for free. Adobe Express has none of these loops firing at scale. The strategy below builds them in three stages.

---

### Now (0–30 days) — Stop losing users before they evaluate

**Fix the free tier template signal — counter: Canva's 1.6M free templates**
- What Canva does: Free users access 1.6 million templates instantly, no credit card, no friction. Users find what they need in seconds and form a habit before they ever consider paying.
- What to do now: Audit Express's free tier template discovery. The problem is not just volume — it's findability. Add AI-powered intent search to the free tier today: a user types "Q2 product launch, LinkedIn banner, clean tech brand" and gets the 5 most relevant Express templates ranked by relevance. This compresses the perceived gap without building 1.4M more templates.
- Target metric: Free tier template-to-first-export rate (if a user finds a usable template, they stay; if they don't, they leave within 90 seconds)

**Activate dormant CC subscribers — counter: Microsoft Designer's zero-friction discovery**
- What Microsoft does: Designer appears inside Teams, Outlook, and PowerPoint. M365 users discover it without downloading anything or opening a new tab. It's embedded in the workflow.
- What to do now: Ship a "Publish to Adobe Express" button in the Photoshop export menu and an "Open in Express" option in Illustrator's Share panel. This is not a marketing campaign — it's an in-product trigger that fires at the exact moment a CC subscriber would otherwise open Canva. Target: the 30M+ CC All Apps subscribers who have never opened Express.
- Target metric: New Express activations attributed to PS/AI in-product entry points (track with UTM parameter `source=photoshop_export`)

**Remove the forgettable first session — counter: Canva's 3-minute time-to-first-value**
- What Canva does: A new user can go from signup → template → exported design in under 3 minutes. The first session creates something shareable.
- What to do now: Audit Express's new user onboarding. Map the current time-to-first-completed-export. Identify the single biggest drop-off point (typically: account creation friction, template discovery confusion, or export format confusion). Fix only that one step this sprint.
- Target metric: Time to first export for new users (current vs. post-fix). Canva's benchmark is ~3 minutes.

### 3 Months — Build the loops that make users bring users

**Loop 1: Output sharing → new user acquisition (counter: Canva's sharing virality)**

Canva's most powerful PLG loop: when a user shares a Canva design publicly (a poster, a social post, a presentation), every viewer sees "Made in Canva" and a link. Canva estimates 30%+ of new signups come from shared design views. Adobe Express has no equivalent.

- Build: Every Express design shared via public link gets a viewer page with a persistent "Create yours free with Adobe Express" CTA in the bottom right. The CTA includes a one-click "start from this template" flow — viewer sees the output, likes it, clicks once, lands in Express with the same template pre-loaded. No signup friction until they try to export.
- Why this works against Canva: Express's Firefly-generated design quality is often higher than Canva's default outputs. Once a viewer sees an Express design, the quality speaks. The "start from this template" flow makes Adobe Express the easier choice at the moment of highest intent.
- Target metric: Share-link viewer → Express signup rate (target: 5%+ in 90 days; industry benchmark for best-in-class is 7–10%)

**Loop 2: Collaboration invite → team expansion (counter: Canva Teams' invite loop)**

Canva grows inside companies because one user invites a teammate → teammate creates an account → team discovers Teams plan → company upgrades. Adobe Express has a collaboration feature but the invite flow is buried and under-promoted.

- Build: After a user shares an Express project for review, show a prominent in-product prompt: "Your reviewer will need a free Adobe account to comment. Invite them now." Surface this at the moment of share, not buried in settings. Track invited teammate activation rate. Build a "your team is already using Express" notification that fires when 3+ users from the same company email domain activate accounts — this triggers the Teams plan upgrade conversation without a sales call.
- Target metric: Invite-to-activation rate (target: 30%+); same-domain multi-user → Teams upgrade conversion

**Loop 3: Firefly safety as a viral B2B signal (counter: Canva's enterprise brand compliance)**

Canva's Q1 2026 AI brand compliance launch is a direct attack on Express's enterprise positioning. Counter with a loop: make Firefly safety visible and shareable inside the product.

- Build: Add a "Firefly Safe" badge to any design that uses only Firefly-generated content — visible on the public share page. When a marketing director shares an Express campaign brief with legal for approval, legal sees the badge and the Firefly IP indemnification statement. This turns a compliance feature into a viral enterprise signal: legal teams start requesting Express specifically. One legal approval creates a departmental mandate.
- Target metric: Number of enterprise accounts where Express adoption started with a legal/compliance stakeholder referral (track via account creation domain analysis)

**Loop 4: ChatGPT integration → new user discovery at scale**

Adobe Express launched inside ChatGPT in December 2025 — 800M ChatGPT users can now access Express. This is the single largest PLG distribution advantage Adobe Express has that no competitor can match today.

- Build: Optimize the ChatGPT → Express conversion flow. When a ChatGPT user generates an Express design, the experience must deliver a completed, shareable output in one session — not a half-finished template that requires Express expertise. Measure the prompt-to-completed-export rate inside ChatGPT. Make it higher than the direct Express onboarding rate (the bar is lower because ChatGPT users already know what they want).
- Target metric: ChatGPT plugin activations → Express account creation rate; prompt-to-export completion rate within the ChatGPT plugin

### 6 Months — Build the moats competitors can't copy

**Moat 1: Creative Cloud template ecosystem — the community flywheel Canva fears most**

Canva's template moat is user-generated. Adobe has 30M+ Creative Cloud subscribers who are professional designers. If 1% of CC subscribers publish one Express template per month, that's 300,000 professional-quality templates added monthly — templates that are better designed than Canva's community contributions because they come from professional designers, not novices.

- Build: Express Template Marketplace — CC subscribers can publish templates, get credit on their Adobe profile, and optionally earn from template downloads (rev-share model). Non-CC users can use the templates but are prompted to upgrade to Express Premium to unlock all of them.
- Why it compounds: Each new CC publisher adds value for all Express users. The marketplace creates a reason for CC subscribers to open Express (to publish and build their Adobe portfolio) and a reason for non-CC users to try Express (to access high-quality templates). Unlike Canva's community templates, Adobe's come from verified professional designers — a quality signal Canva cannot replicate.
- Competitive defense: Canva's template community is 8 years old. Adobe has the professional designer base to close this gap in 18–24 months if the program launches now.
- Target metric: Templates published/month; organic search traffic from template discovery pages; Express signups attributed to template search

**Moat 2: Acrobat Studio as the enterprise PLG entry point**

Adobe Express's biggest untapped PLG lever is the Acrobat install base. Acrobat has 500M+ users globally. Acrobat Studio (launched August 2025) combines Acrobat + Express in one platform. Every time an Acrobat user converts a Word doc to PDF, edits a scanned contract, or sends a document for e-signature, there is now an opportunity to surface Express.

- Build: When an Acrobat user exports a PDF to share externally (e.g., a proposal, a report cover), show an in-product prompt: "Make this look better before you send. Open in Adobe Express." One click — the PDF cover page opens in Express with pre-applied brand colors. The user redesigns it in 2 minutes and exports. They've now used Express for the first time without ever deciding to try a new tool.
- Why this compounds: Acrobat is in every enterprise. Express rides Acrobat's install base at zero additional acquisition cost. This is a distribution advantage Canva, Figma, and Microsoft Designer cannot replicate — none of them have a PDF workflow at Acrobat's scale.
- Target metric: Express activations attributed to Acrobat Studio in-product prompts; Acrobat-to-Express cross-usage rate per account

**Moat 3: Firefly AI flywheel — quality improves as Adobe Express usage grows**

Every design created in Adobe Express with Firefly is a training signal. The more Express is used, the better Firefly's outputs become for specific design contexts (social banners, marketing emails, presentation covers). Competitors using the same base model (DALL-E, Stable Diffusion) without this usage signal produce measurably lower-quality outputs for these specific use cases over time.

- Build: Structured feedback loop inside Express — when a user regenerates a Firefly output (a signal they didn't like the first result), capture that as negative training signal. When a user exports a Firefly-generated design without modification (a signal the first result was good), capture that as positive. Fine-tune Firefly's Express-specific models quarterly on this signal.
- Why it compounds: AI model quality from usage data is invisible to competitors and takes years to replicate. By the time a competitor has the same volume of structured design feedback, Adobe's model is two years ahead. This is the flywheel that makes the Express + Firefly combination increasingly unbeatable for commercial design quality.
- Target metric: First-generation acceptance rate (% of Firefly outputs used without regeneration) — track quarterly; target improvement of 5% per quarter
