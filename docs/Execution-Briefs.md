## Brief 1 — "PDF Editor Free Trial" SEO Landing Page

**Motion from playbook:** Build a dedicated landing page at acrobat.adobe.com/free-trial targeting "PDF editor free" (200K/mo searches). Adobe is invisible for this keyword; Foxit ranks #1 with a no-credit-card trial page.

**Competitor countered:** Foxit (ranks #1–2 for this keyword, no CC required on trial)

---

### Landing Page Brief

**URL:** `acrobat.adobe.com/free-trial`

**Page title (for SEO + GEO):** `Try Adobe Acrobat Free — No Credit Card Required`

**Meta description:** `Start your free 7-day Acrobat Pro trial. Edit, sign, compress, and share PDFs. No credit card needed. Cancel anytime.`

**H1:** `The world's most trusted PDF editor. Free for 7 days.`

**Hero subheadline:** `Edit, sign, compress, and redact PDFs. No credit card. No commitment. Just Acrobat.`

**Primary CTA button:** `Start Free Trial — No Credit Card`

**Trust line below CTA:** `7-day full access · Cancel anytime · No ETF on trial`

**3 proof points (below fold):**
- `Trusted by 500M+ users worldwide`
- `Acrobat Sign included — send documents for signature free during trial`
- `Rated 4.6/5 on G2 across 3,000+ reviews`

**Pricing comparison table on page:**

| | Adobe Acrobat | Foxit | UPDF |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free trial | 7 days, full access | 14 days | 14 days |
| Credit card for trial | No | No | No |
| eSign included | ✅ | Add-on | ❌ |
| AI Assistant | ✅ | Limited | Basic |
| Annual price | $239.88 | $159.99 | $29.99 |

---

### In-Product / Retargeting Email

**Subject line A:** `You haven't tried Acrobat yet — here's 7 free days, no card needed`  
**Subject line B:** `Foxit is $80 cheaper. Here's why Acrobat users don't switch.`

**Email body (3 sentences):**
> Adobe Acrobat Pro is free for 7 days — no credit card, no commitment. During your trial you get full access to PDF editing, Acrobat Sign (send unlimited signature requests), AI document summarization, and 100GB cloud storage. Start your trial in 30 seconds at acrobat.adobe.com/free-trial.

**CTA:** `Start Free Trial`

---

### A/B Test Hypothesis

**Control:** Current trial page (requires CC, generic headline)  
**Variant:** New page with "No Credit Card" in H1 and above the fold, and an explicit "vs Foxit" pricing table  
**Hypothesis:** Removing CC requirement + surfacing competitor price comparison will increase trial starts by 40%+ for users arriving via non-branded PDF editor searches  
**Primary metric:** Trial starts from non-branded organic search  
**Secondary metric:** Trial-to-paid conversion rate (to ensure no quality degradation)  
**Run time:** 4 weeks minimum, 10K+ visits per variant

---

### Success Metric

Non-branded organic trial starts: **500/mo → 5,000/mo within 90 days**

## Brief 2 — Signer-to-Trial CTA on Acrobat Sign Emails

**Motion from playbook:** 200M+ free signers receive Acrobat-signed documents monthly and see zero conversion prompt. Add a single CTA below the "document signed" confirmation.

**Competitor countered:** This is a free acquisition channel unique to Adobe — no competitor can replicate it.

**Body (1 sentence):** `Adobe Acrobat lets you send, track, and manage unlimited signature requests — try it free for 7 days.`

**CTA button:** `Start Sending for Free`

**Trust line:** `No credit card · 7-day full trial · Used by 500M+ people`

---

**Subject:** `You just signed a document with Adobe. Want to send one?`

**Body:**
> Yesterday you signed a document sent via Adobe Acrobat Sign. Thousands of businesses use Acrobat to send contracts, approvals, and agreements for signature every day — and you can too.
>
> Start a free 7-day trial. No credit card. Send your first document in under 5 minutes.

**CTA:** `Try Acrobat Free`

---

**Control:** No CTA on signer confirmation screen  
**Variant A:** CTA below confirmation, before "Download a copy"  
**Variant B:** CTA as a separate follow-up email 24h after signing

## Brief 3 — FTC Narrative Reclaim: Paid + Organic Campaign

**Motion from playbook:** Prospects researching "Adobe Acrobat review" hit the FTC lawsuit story before they hit product content. Foxit runs ads on "Adobe Acrobat ETF" and "Adobe Acrobat cancel." Adobe cedes this traffic entirely.

**Competitor countered:** Foxit (actively running "no lock-in" ads on Adobe's brand problem keywords)

---

### Blog Post Brief

**URL:** `blog.adobe.com/acrobat/our-subscription-terms-explained`  
**Title:** `Adobe Acrobat Subscription Terms — Explained Plainly`  
**Target query:** "Adobe Acrobat cancel subscription" (40K/mo), "Adobe Acrobat ETF" (15K/mo)

**Structure:**
1. What the annual plan includes and costs ($239.88/yr)
2. What happens if you cancel in year 1 (ETF — acknowledge it directly, explain why it exists)
3. What the month-to-month option costs ($22.99/mo, no ETF)
4. How to cancel (step-by-step, 3 steps, no phone call required)
5. What changed since the FTC case (process improvements)

**Tone:** Radically transparent. No legal hedging. First person ("we"). This page will be cited by AI assistants — clarity is more important than caution.

---

## Brief 4 — AI Intent Search for Templates

**Motion from playbook:** Replace keyword-only template search with natural language: "I need a LinkedIn post announcing a product launch in tech" → ranked templates + AI previews. Directly counters Canva's #1 acquisition advantage.

**Competitor countered:** Canva (ranks #1 for every design template keyword; their discoverability is the primary reason new users choose them)

---

### Feature Copy Brief

**Empty state headline (before search):** `What are you making today?`

**Search placeholder text:** `Try "Instagram post for a coffee shop" or "pitch deck for a SaaS startup"`

**Zero-results state:** `We didn't find an exact match — here are 5 templates our AI thinks will work. Customize any of them.`

**AI-matched template card label:** `AI Pick · Based on your prompt`

**Onboarding tooltip (first use):** `Adobe Express understands what you're trying to make — not just keywords. Try describing your goal in plain English.`

---

### Onboarding Card Copy (replaces blank canvas)

**Headline:** `What are you making?`

**3 questions (shown as tap cards):**
1. `Who's your audience?` → Social media · Business · Personal · Education
2. `What type of content?` → Social post · Presentation · Flyer · Video · Logo · Document
3. `What's the goal?` → Announce something · Promote a product · Share an update · Just exploring

**After 3 taps:** Show 5 template recommendations with "Start with this" CTA on each

**Skip link:** `Start with a blank canvas →`

---

### A/B Test Hypothesis

**Control:** Current keyword search + blank canvas default  
**Variant:** Natural language search + 3-question onboarding card  
**Hypothesis:** Intent-matched templates reduce blank canvas drop-off and increase first-session export rate by 2×  
**Primary metric:** First-session template selection rate (35% → 65%)  
**Secondary metric:** Time-to-first-export (8–12 min → under 3 min)  
**Run time:** 3 weeks, new users only

---

### Success Metric

First-session creation completion: **28% → 55%**

## Brief 9 — Remove → Generative Fill Upsell Modal

**Motion from playbook:** After every background removal, show a post-removal modal with 5 AI-generated scene options (Generative Fill). Extends the session from 30 seconds to 3–5 minutes and is the natural free-to-paid conversion trigger.

**Competitor countered:** All competitors — no other background removal tool offers AI scene replacement. This is Adobe-only.

---

### Modal Copy Brief

**Trigger:** Fires immediately after background removal completes, before download

**Modal headline:** `What's behind your subject?`

**Subheadline:** `Generate a background with AI — studio, lifestyle, product shot, or custom scene.`

**5 preset scene buttons:**
1. `White studio`
2. `Lifestyle — outdoor`
3. `Product on shelf`
4. `Gradient minimal`
5. `Describe your own...`

**After selecting a preset:** Preview renders in 3 seconds with Generative Fill  
**CTA below preview:** `Use this background`  
**Secondary:** `Try another scene`  
**Tertiary (dismiss):** `Just download the cutout`

**If user is on free plan and has used 5+ Generative Fill credits:**  
**Upsell headline:** `You've discovered Adobe's most powerful feature`  
**Body:** `Unlimited AI backgrounds with Adobe Express — $8.33/mo, less than one remove.bg subscription.`  
**CTA:** `Unlock unlimited AI`

---

### A/B Test Hypothesis

**Control:** Download button appears immediately after removal  
**Variant:** Post-removal modal with 5 Generative Fill scene previews  
**Hypothesis:** Users who see the Generative Fill upsell immediately after removal will activate at 4× the rate of users who go straight to download  
**Primary metric:** Remove → Generative Fill activation rate  
**Secondary metric:** Free-to-paid conversion rate among users who see the modal  
**Run time:** 3 weeks

---

### Success Metric

Remove → Generative Fill activation: **3% → 12%**  
This is the primary free-to-paid trigger for the Remove Background acquisition funnel
